Sources & Methods
This project draws on publicly available data from Statistics Canada, the Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation (CMHC), and the Bank of Canada. All figures are presented in inflation-adjusted (constant) dollars where indicated to allow for comparisons over time.

Housing
Statistics Canada — National Household Survey (2011) and Census (2016, 2021)
CMHC — Housing Supply Report (2022; updated 2025 estimates)

Prices
Statistics Canada — Consumer Price Index (CPI), table 18-10-0004-01

Wages
Statistics Canada — Labour Force Survey, real median hourly wages

Education
Statistics Canada — Tuition fees for degree programs, table 37-10-0003-01
National comparisons are presented in constant dollars to reflect real changes over time

Wealth
Statistics Canada — Survey of Financial Security (1999–2023)

Policy Context
Bank of Canada — Housing affordability indicators and policy rate data
CMHC — Estimates of housing supply required to restore affordability

Methodology

Where possible, comparisons are made between Canadians at the same age across time rather than between generations. This approach helps isolate structural economic changes from normal life-cycle effects, since wealth and homeownership tend to rise with age.

All long-run comparisons are inflation-adjusted to constant dollars. Calculations such as hours of work required to pay tuition are author calculations based on publicly reported figures.

Limitations

National averages may mask significant regional variation, particularly in housing markets such as Toronto and Vancouver. Changes in survey methods over time may also affect comparability across years. Generational labels are used as a narrative tool, but underlying analysis is based on age cohorts.
